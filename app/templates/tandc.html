Section 1 Technology

1.1 The Client agrees to purchase the Solar Technology from the Supplier and for the Supplier to install the Solar Technology at the Property on the terms of this Agreement and the Client agrees to be bound by this Agreement on and from Acceptance.

1.2 The Supplier agrees to install the Solar Technology on the Property in a good and professional manner and be bound by this Agreement on and from Acceptance.

1.3 The Supplier reserves its right to terminate this Agreement for any reason within 5 business days of Acceptance.

Section 2 Purchase Price and Amount Payable

2.1 The Client agrees to pay the Deposit of 10% of the Price to the Supplier on or forthwith after Acceptance.

2.2 The Deposit is forfeited to the Supplier if this Agreement is terminated as a result of the Clients default.

2.3 Advertised amounts are subject to logistical variation.

2.4 The Client agrees to pay the progress payments as follows. a. 40% of the Price on Completion of the installation of the solar equipment prior to commissioning. (Progress Payments) b. Completion of each stage will occur on the date on which the Supplier provides written notification to the Client of completion of the stage together with an interim invoice for payment of that stage.

2.5 The balance of the Price (Price less the Deposit and Progress Payments) must be paid to the Supplier upon Final completion

2.6 The Client agrees that any changes made by the Client to the scope of works set out in this contact or construction drawings supplied to the Client by the Supplier at time of signing will be at additional cost to the Client.

2.7 Unless otherwise stated in the quotation, any preparatory site works including demolition, excavation, earthmoving or trenching shall be the responsibility of the Client and as such the Client will be responsible for any costs incurred therein.

2.8 Unless otherwise stated in the quotation, The Client is responsible for coordination of the smart/net/solar meter upgrade with their electricity retailer. The Supplier will provide all necessary documents to the Client for this purpose.

Section 3 Authority to Install

3.1 The Client authorizes the Supplier to install the Solar Technology at the Property.

3.2 The person(s) signing this Agreement warrants that they have full authority to enter into this Agreement. The Client must ensure that the Supplier has sufficient access to the Property at the times it may reasonably require in order to install or remove the Solar Technology. If, during the process of installation, some or all of the Solar Technology are affixed, attached to the Property or any building on the Property, the goods are deemed not to be a fixture and may be removed by the Supplier at any time in accordance with this Agreement.

3.3 The Client must sign all documents and take all action the Supplier requires to install the Solar Technology.

Section 4 Authority to Remove on Termination and Costs Payable on Termination

4.1 If this Agreement is terminated because of the Clients default, the Supplier may remove the Solar Technology from the Property if any amount remains due and payable to the Supplier under this Agreement 15 days after the date of termination.

4.2 The proceeds from any sale of Solar Technology (net of the cost of their removal and sale) may be set off against any amount due from the Client to The Supplier under this Agreement.

4.3 If the Client lists or sells the property after Acceptance and there is money payable by the Client to the Supplier, then the Client agrees that such money outstanding is secured over the Property and the Supplier is entitled to lodge a caveat over the title of the Property.

5.1 Ownership of Solar Technology on the Client’s property passes to the Client after the Client has: a. Paid the Price in full to the Supplier; and b. Completed any documents, or taken any action that The Supplier requires under this Agreement.

5.3 Except as agreed herein or required by law, all express and implied warranties are excluded and The Supplier shall not be liable for any physical injury, financial loss or damage (whether direct or consequential) arising from the supply & installation of the Solar Technology.

7.1 The Supplier may terminate this Agreement if the Client fails to comply with its terms.

7.4 If the Client notifies the Supplier in writing of the Clients decision to terminate this Agreement pursuant to clause 7.2 then the Client’s Deposit will be refunded to the Client within 30 days of receipt of such notification and the Solar Technology can be used by the Supplier for another customer.
