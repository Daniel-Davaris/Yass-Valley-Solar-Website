  • Battery Storage

    The best in battery technology.

    Our systems provide a cost-effective alternative to Grid Power for many locations where it is expensive to connect to the grid or for people looking for a more reliable self-sufficient power solution. These systems can accommodate for those with even larger power requirements by utilizing AC Coupling Technology which enables daytime loads to be supplied directly from the Solar Array which minimizes the draw on the battery system and gives more capacity for night-time loads.

    Lead Acid Gel (VRLA)
    The battery of choice for off grid systems with unrivalled reliability and a long, maintenance free lifespan, capable of delivering large amounts of power.

    Absorbent Glass Matt (AGM)
    Lead acid battery with a lower price point, well suited to smaller systems with periodic usage.

    Lithium Iron Phosphate (LiFePO4)
    New technology with a similar lifespan to lead acid gel batteries that provide greater flexibly in the incremental addition of new batteries.

    Flow Batteries
    Alternative to traditional battery types with the ability to discharge without damage and without loss of capacity over their lifespan at the cost of affordability.

  • Energy Efficiency

    Heating Systems

    Wood heating is traditionally the most common form of heating for an off-grid house. ... Wood provides a cost-effective heating solution as it requires no electricity to run and can produce very large amounts of heat compared to other heating sources. Positioning of the fire is important and there are various options available to transfer heat to other areas of the house. A good quality fire will burn much more efficiently and enable longer run-times over night without loading in more timber.

    Geothermal Hydronic heating is possibly the ultimate heating source. By extracting heat from the ground using a series of ground loops this option provides the most efficient of all electric heating systems. ... Hot water is then piped through the concrete slab or through radiator panels to heat the house. The key to an efficient hydronic heating system is using the thermal mass of the building, typically the slab, to retain the heat allowing operation of the hydronics during the day from the solar system whilst keeping the house at a constant temperature during the night. This helps control the cost of an off-grid system by minimizing the amount of battery storage needed for the heating. Modern hydronic heat pumps also have the option to run ducted cooling systems.

    An air sourced hydronic heat pump works on the same principle as a geothermal heat pump, however it draws the heat from the air rather than the ground. ... As the air temperature is more variable and typically much lower that the ground temperature in winter, the air sourced heat pump operates at a lower efficiency. That being said, newer heat pumps are becoming more and more efficient and have a price advantage over the geothermal systems.

    Hydronic heating using a wood, chip or gas fed boiler unit provides all the benefits of a hydronic system without the use of any significant amount of electricity. ... A good design is key to making these systems work well and we have seen many examples of these systems providing extraordinary results.

    Gas heating A free-standing gas heater can provide a great supplementary heating potion solution. ... While they are generally ineffective at heating large areas, they provide great instant heat and can be an ideal addition to say, a wood heated house for times when the wood heater isn't running etc. Pros: Itâ€™s low electricity consumption and high levels of heat output can suit an off-grid solar system. Cons: Cost of bottled LPG gas can make for a high ongoing cost where natural gas is not available.

    Electric split system air conditioners. When used for cooling, they operate extremely efficiently. ... In terms of heating, they have limited use as they are less efficient during cool outside temperatures. As they work on heating the air rather than a thermal mass, the inside temperature drops quickly as soon as the unit is turned off. Split system air conditioners have their place for cooling and small amounts of heating in-between seasons in many off-grid homes

    Hot Water

    Solar Gas Boosted hot water is the most common off-grid hot water system available and provides consistent operation while using minimal amounts of power and keeping gas costs low.

    Solar Electric Boosted water can be used for lower use applications or where there is a surplus amount of solar energy during the winter months.

    Gas Instantaneous hot water provides the lowest installation costs and can provide unlimited amounts of hot water, however this system has a higher running cost compared to the other options.

    Heat Pump hot water systems provide a gas free solution and have a much lower power usage than a traditional hot water system. This option will however, require additional capacity in the off-grid system to enable operation.
